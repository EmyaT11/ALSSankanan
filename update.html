ALS 2.0

The Department of Education (DepEd) is continuously updating and improving the Alternative Learning System (ALS) program, with a focus on making it more accessible, relevant, and inclusive for out-of-school youth and adults. Recent updates include the implementation of the ALS 2.0 Strategic Roadmap, which aims to enhance the program's quality and relevance, and the development of resources like the ALS ICT Strategic Plan and the ALS Research Agenda.

1.ALS 2.0 Strategic Roadmap:

ALS 2.0 Strategic Roadmap: This roadmap focuses on redesigning the ALS to improve its quality and relevance, equipping teachers with better tools, and providing learners with practical knowledge.

2.ALS ICT Strategic Plan (2022-2026):

This plan outlines the role of Information and Communication Technology (ICT) in improving access, quality, and governance within the ALS program.

3.ALS Research Agenda (2022-2026):

This agenda promotes evidence-based decision-making and policy development for the ALS program, aiming for quality and inclusive lifelong learning.

4.ICT4ALS:

This website provides online resources and apps for ALS learners, teachers, and implementers, particularly valuable during the pandemic and beyond.

5.DepEd Orders and Issuances:

DepEd has issued several orders related to ALS, including guidelines on the release, utilization, monitoring, and reporting of program support funds, as well as guidelines on the allocation and use of support funds for regional offices.

6.Bureau of Alternative Education (BAE):

DepEd established the BAE to be the focal office for implementing the ALS program and to strengthen and expand its reach to various groups, including indigenous peoples, persons deprived of liberty, and learners with disabilities.

7.Accreditation and Equivalency (A&E) Program:

The ALS program also includes the A&E program, which provides learners with the opportunity to complete basic education through non-formal means.

8.Emphasis on Lifelong Learning:

The ALS program aims to provide learners with the skills and knowledge they need for continued learning and to enhance their employability.

9.Focus on Post-Program Support:

The ALS program is also working to provide learners with support after they complete the program, including guidance on career paths and opportunities for higher education or vocational training.

10.Challenges and Solutions:

The ALS program faces challenges like low enrollment and completion rates, but ongoing initiatives are being implemented to address these issues, such as providing more accessible learning materials, expanding the use of ICT, and improving post-program support.

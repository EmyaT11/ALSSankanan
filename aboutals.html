What is AlS

The Alternative Learning System (ALS) in the Philippines is a parallel learning system designed to provide basic education to individuals who have not been able to access or complete formal education. It offers a practical, alternative pathway for out-of-school youth and adults, including those with literacy gaps. ALS includes both non-formal and informal learning sources and aims to improve literacy skills, promote equivalent pathways to basic education, and enhance the quality of life for individuals who need a second chance at education.

Parallel System:

ALS is a parallel system to the formal education system, providing an alternative learning pathway for individuals who cannot access or complete formal education.

Target Audience:

ALS primarily serves out-of-school children, youth, and adults (OSCYAs), including those who have dropped out of school, have never attended school, or need to improve their literacy skills.

Community-Based Learning:

ALS often emphasizes community-based learning, promoting self-help and community development.
